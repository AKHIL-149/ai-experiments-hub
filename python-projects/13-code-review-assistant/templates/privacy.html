Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how the AI Code Review & Refactoring Assistant ("Service", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects your information when you use our Service.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your code and personal information.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Username: Your chosen username (3-50 characters)
  • Email Address: Used for account verification, notifications, and communication
  • Password: Stored as a bcrypt hash (we never store plaintext passwords)
  • Account Creation Date: Timestamp of when you created your account

2.2 Code and Repository Data

When you use the Service, we collect and process:

  • Source Code: Code files you upload or repositories you connect
  • Repository Information: Repository names, URLs, branch names
  • Pull Request Data: PR titles, descriptions, diffs, and metadata
  • Analysis Results: Issues detected, metrics, and AI-generated suggestions
  • Code Metadata: File names, line counts, language types, complexity metrics

2.3 GitHub Integration Data

When you connect your GitHub account, we collect:

  • GitHub Token: Personal access token or GitHub App installation token (encrypted)
  • Repository Access: Information about repositories you grant access to
  • Webhook Events: PR opened, synchronized, closed events
  • GitHub Username: Your GitHub username and user ID

2.4 Usage Information

We automatically collect certain information about your use of the Service:

  • Session Data: Login times, IP addresses, session duration
  • Activity Logs: Actions performed (file uploads, PR imports, analyses)
  • Performance Metrics: Analysis times, API response times
  • Error Logs: Error messages and stack traces (with sensitive data masked)

2.5 Technical Information

  • IP Address: For security and rate limiting
  • Browser Information: User agent, browser type and version
  • Device Information: Operating system, device type
  • Cookies: Session cookies for authentication

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 To Provide the Service

  • Analyze your code for issues, vulnerabilities, and quality metrics
  • Generate AI-powered suggestions and refactorings
  • Integrate with GitHub for PR reviews and webhooks
  • Store and display analysis results and history
  • Enable team collaboration and analytics features

3.2 To Improve the Service

  • Monitor and analyze usage patterns to improve features
  • Debug and fix errors
  • Optimize performance and scalability
  • Develop new analysis rules and capabilities

3.3 To Communicate With You

  • Send email notifications (if enabled)
  • Send Slack notifications (if configured)
  • Notify you of important service updates or security issues
  • Respond to your support requests

3.4 For Security and Compliance

  • Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security threats
  • Enforce our Terms of Service
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Audit logs for security investigations

4. How We Share Your Information

4.1 Third-Party AI Providers

We may share your code with AI providers for analysis:

  • Ollama: If you use local Ollama models, code is processed locally
  • Anthropic Claude: If enabled, code snippets are sent to Anthropic for analysis
  • OpenAI: If enabled, code snippets are sent to OpenAI for analysis

You control which AI provider is used through your configuration. Code sent to external AI providers is subject to their privacy policies.

4.2 Service Providers

We may share information with service providers who assist us in operating the Service:

  • Cloud hosting providers (for infrastructure)
  • Email delivery services (for notifications)
  • Monitoring and analytics services

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to:

  • Comply with legal obligations or court orders
  • Protect our rights, property, or safety
  • Investigate fraud or security issues
  • Respond to government requests

4.4 With Your Consent

We may share your information with third parties when you give us explicit consent to do so.

4.5 Team Members

If you create or join a team, team members can view:

  • Shared repositories and analysis results
  • Team analytics and metrics
  • Team member activity (depending on permissions)

5. Data Security

5.1 Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted over HTTPS/TLS
  • Encryption at Rest: Sensitive data encrypted in database
  • Password Security: Passwords hashed with bcrypt
  • Token Security: GitHub tokens and API keys encrypted
  • Access Control: Role-based access control (RBAC)
  • Rate Limiting: Protection against brute force attacks
  • Security Headers: CSP, HSTS, X-Frame-Options, etc.
  • Input Validation: All inputs validated and sanitized
  • Audit Logging: Security-relevant actions logged

5.2 Data Retention

  • Account Data: Retained while your account is active
  • Code and Analysis Results: Retained until you delete them or close your account
  • Logs: Retained for 90 days for security and debugging
  • Deleted Data: Permanently deleted within 30 days of account closure

6. Your Rights and Choices

6.1 Access and Update

You can access and update your account information through the Service settings.

6.2 Delete Your Account

You can delete your account at any time. This will permanently delete:

  • Your account information
  • Your uploaded code and repositories
  • All analysis results and history
  • Your team memberships (teams with only you will be deleted)

6.3 Export Your Data

You can export your analysis data in JSON or CSV format through the analytics dashboard.

6.4 Opt-Out of Notifications

You can disable email and Slack notifications in your settings at any time.

6.5 Revoke GitHub Access

You can disconnect your GitHub account or revoke repository access through your GitHub settings or through the Service.

6.6 Data Portability

You can request a copy of your data in a machine-readable format by contacting us.

7. Cookies and Tracking

7.1 Cookies We Use

  • Session Cookies: Essential for authentication (cannot be disabled)
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings (theme, language)

7.2 Third-Party Cookies

We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for users under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have different data protection laws. By using the Service, you consent to the transfer of your information to these countries.

10. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information or code, we will:

  • Notify affected users via email within 72 hours
  • Describe the nature of the breach and data affected
  • Outline steps we are taking to address the breach
  • Provide recommendations for protecting your account
  • Comply with applicable data breach notification laws

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the new Privacy Policy on this page
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Sending an email notification (for significant changes)

Your continued use of the Service after changes indicates your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

12. GDPR Compliance (For EU Users)

If you are in the European Union, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Restriction: Restrict processing of your personal data
  • Right to Portability: Receive your data in a portable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing of your personal data
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@codereviewassistant.example.com

13. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under CCPA:

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to know if personal information is sold or disclosed
  • Right to opt-out of sale of personal information
  • Right to deletion of personal information
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights

Note: We do not sell your personal information.
